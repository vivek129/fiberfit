Scientific Research

Modern studies validate the traditional wisdom about millet health benefits

Diabetes Management

A 2021 study published in the Journal of Food Science and Technology found that regular consumption of millets reduced HbA1c levels by an average of 12% in type 2 diabetes patients.

Key Finding: Millets have a low glycemic index (GI) ranging from 35-55, compared to white rice (GI 73) and wheat (GI 65).

Cardiovascular Health

Research from the International Journal of Molecular Sciences (2022) demonstrates that millet consumption is associated with reduced LDL cholesterol and improved lipid profiles.

Key Finding: The high magnesium content in millets supports heart health and helps regulate blood pressure naturally.

Weight Management

A comprehensive review in Nutrients (2023) showed that millet-based diets led to significant weight loss and improved metabolic markers in overweight individuals.

Key Finding: High fiber and protein content promotes satiety and reduces overall calorie intake without compromising nutrition.

Bone Health

Studies on ragi (finger millet) published in the Journal of Nutritional Science (2021) highlight its exceptional calcium content and bioavailability for bone health.

Key Finding: Ragi contains 10x more calcium than rice and wheat, making it ideal for preventing osteoporosis and supporting bone development.
